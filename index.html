World Brain Day is observed every year on July 22nd to raise global awareness about the importance of brain health.

The initiative was launched by the World Federation of Neurology in 2014 to commemorate its founding in 1957.

The primary goal is to highlight neurological disorders as a major public health challenge worldwide.

Each year, the campaign adopts a specific theme; in 2025 it’s “Brain Health for All Ages.”

This theme emphasizes protection and promotion of healthy brains from infancy through old age.

World Brain Day unites healthcare professionals, patients, caregivers, and policy-makers.

Events include webinars, workshops, community screenings, and social-media campaigns under #WorldBrainDay.

Preventive measures such as balanced nutrition, regular exercise, quality sleep and mental workouts are promoted.

Early detection of stroke, dementia, epilepsy, Parkinson’s and other conditions can save lives.

Collaborators such as WHO and UN ECOSOC support the campaign under the IGAP framework.

The 2025 effort focuses on improving neurological care access in low- and middle-income countries.

Millions globally suffer from brain disorders—many lack timely diagnosis and treatment.

On this day, advocacy groups distribute toolkits, posters and online resources to raise awareness.

Local chapters of the WFN host fundraisers, screenings, and educational walks.

Researchers share breakthroughs in neuroscience to foster global collaboration.

The campaign calls on governments to allocate more funding and policy support for brain research.

Healthy lifestyle choices are shown to reduce the risk of neurological disease.

School programs teach children about brain anatomy, function, and care.

Workplace seminars emphasize stress management and cognitive health.

July 22nd reminds us that brain health underpins every aspect of well-being.
